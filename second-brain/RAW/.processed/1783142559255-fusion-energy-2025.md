## Key Findings

According to the International Atomic Energy Agency, fusion energy progress is accelerating in 2025, with private investment surpassing $10 billion and fusion poised to play a substantial role in future electricity generation.

## Notable Breakthroughs

1. **Commonwealth Fusion Systems** — Advancing its SPARC pilot plant outside Boston, scheduled to come online in 2027. The facility's tokamak design uses high-temperature superconducting magnets.

2. **April 2025 Demonstration** — A fusion device delivered 8.6 MJ of energy, more than four times the 2.08 MJ input energy required for ignition. This represents a significant milestone in net energy gain.

3. **Commercial Viability** — Increasing confidence that commercial fusion is approaching, with several facilities targeting operational status within the next decade.
