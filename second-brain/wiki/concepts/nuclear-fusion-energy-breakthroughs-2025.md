## Summary

The year 2025 marked a significant acceleration in fusion energy development, with private investment exceeding $10 billion and notable breakthroughs demonstrating the path toward commercial viability. The International Atomic Energy Agency reported substantial progress in fusion technology, with Commonwealth Fusion Systems advancing its SPARC pilot plant scheduled for 2027 operation. A particularly significant milestone was achieved in April 2025 when a fusion device delivered 8.6 MJ of energy, more than four times the input energy required for ignition, proving net energy gain at scale. These developments indicate the transition from theoretical promise to practical implementation, with several facilities targeting operational status within the next decade.

## Key Facts

- Private investment in fusion energy surpassed $10 billion in 2025, according to the International Atomic Energy Agency.
- Commonwealth Fusion Systems is advancing its SPARC pilot plant outside Boston, with an operational target of 2027.
- An April 2025 fusion demonstration achieved 8.6 MJ of output energy, exceeding the 2.08 MJ input required for ignition by more than four times.
- This 8.6 MJ output represents a significant milestone in achieving net energy gain from fusion reactions.
- Multiple fusion facilities are targeting operational status within the next decade, indicating growing confidence in commercial viability.
- The SPARC facility uses a tokamak design featuring high-temperature superconducting magnets.
